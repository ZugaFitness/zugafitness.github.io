Online Yoga Classes for Indians in the UK — Live, Not Recorded

Missing your yoga practice in the UK? Zuga Fitness runs live online yoga classes in GMT timezone — taught by Indian instructors who understand your practice.

Zuga Fitness runs live yoga classes in GMT timezone for the Indian diaspora in the UK

Moving to the UK changes a lot of things. Your food, your weather, your routine. And if yoga was part of your life back home — your practice.

Finding a yoga class in the UK that feels familiar is harder than it sounds. Most studios teach a westernised version of yoga that's technically correct but somehow feels disconnected from the tradition you grew up with. And the timings rarely work for someone trying to maintain an Indian routine in a British timezone.

This is exactly the gap Zuga was built to fill.

The Timezone Problem — Solved

GMT is 5.5 hours behind IST. Which means a 6am class in Bangalore is 12:30am in London — completely useless for anyone actually living there.

Zuga runs dedicated live sessions in GMT timezone — timed for mornings and evenings in the UK, not India. So you can practice at a civilised hour, with an Indian instructor, without compromising your sleep.

Current GMT slots include early morning (6:30am GMT) and evening (7pm GMT) — suitable whether you prefer starting your day with yoga or unwinding after work. For those working irregular hours, we also have advice on the best time to do yoga for night shift workers.

Why Indian Instructors Matter

This isn't about nationalism — it's about depth of knowledge.

Yoga originated in India. The philosophical framework, the Sanskrit terminology, the understanding of how Pranayama connects to asana, the respect for individual body types and health conditions — these are things that take years of immersive training to truly understand.

Our instructors Mallinath and Anusha were trained in authentic Indian yoga traditions — not a weekend teacher training course. When they teach Nadi Shodhana or explain the purpose of Savasana, it comes from a place of genuine understanding rather than a textbook.

For Indian students in the UK, this difference is felt immediately.

Small Classes — You're Not Just a Screen

One of the biggest frustrations with Online Group Classes is feeling invisible. A 50-person Zoom class where the instructor barely glances at individual screens defeats the purpose entirely.

Zuga caps every live session at 10 students. Your instructor sees you, knows your name, remembers that your left shoulder is tight or that you're working on your back flexibility. Real-time corrections happen in every class — the same way they would in a physical studio.

This is what students from the UK consistently tell us they were missing before finding Zuga.

What Students in the UK Say

Priya S., who joined from the UK, told us: finding live yoga in her timezone was tough until Zuga. The instructors are amazing.

That's a sentiment we hear regularly from UK-based students — the combination of timing, instruction quality and community is what keeps them coming back.

Styles Available in GMT Timezone

Depending on which slot you join, classes available in GMT timezone include:

  • Hatha — foundational, alignment-focused, ideal for all levels
  • Vinyasa — flowing, breath-linked movement, builds strength and flexibility
  • Yin — deep holds, connective tissue work, meditative
  • Pranayama & Meditation — breathwork sessions, perfect for evenings

The Community Aspect

One of the unexpected benefits students mention is the community. When you're an Indian living in the UK, your Zuga class becomes a familiar space — practicing alongside other Indian students from Dubai, New York, Bangalore and London, led by instructors who share your cultural context.

Several students have told us it's the part of their week that feels most like home. For those dealing with specific health concerns like PCOD Yoga, having a supportive community and expert guidance makes all the difference.
