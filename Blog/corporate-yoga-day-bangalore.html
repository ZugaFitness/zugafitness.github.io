Planning a Yoga Day Session for Your Bangalore Office? Read This First

International Yoga Day falls on June 21 every year. And every year, hundreds of Bangalore companies scramble in the last two weeks to find a yoga trainer for their team — only to discover that the good ones are already booked.

More Bangalore companies are investing in employee wellness on International Yoga Day

If you're an HR manager or office admin reading this, you're ahead of the curve. Here's everything you need to know to plan a session your team will genuinely enjoy.

Why Companies Do This

The short answer: it works, it's visible, and employees remember it.

A 60-minute yoga session costs less than a team lunch. But unlike lunch, it produces a measurable physical and psychological effect — reduced cortisol, improved mood, better focus for the rest of the day. And it signals something important from leadership: we care about how you feel, not just what you produce.

For IT companies specifically — where back pain, eye strain, and burnout are endemic — Yoga Day is increasingly being treated as a serious wellness investment rather than a box-ticking exercise.

Onsite vs Online — Which Is Right for Your Team?

Most Bangalore companies prefer onsite. There's something about a shared physical experience — colleagues on mats next to each other, laughing through their first Warrior Pose — that a Zoom session can't replicate.

Choose onsite if:

  • Your team is largely in-office on June 21
  • You have a usable open space (terrace, lawn, large conference room, cleared floor)
  • Team size is 20–100 people

Choose online if:

  • Your team is distributed or hybrid
  • You want to include remote employees equally
  • Logistics of a physical setup feel complicated

Zuga offers both. Our instructors travel to any location across Bangalore for onsite sessions. Check our Online Group Classes for more information.

What Space Do You Actually Need?

This is the most common question we get. The answer is simpler than most HR teams expect.

Each participant needs roughly a 2m × 1m space — enough to lie down with arms extended. A standard conference room that fits 20 people for a meeting will comfortably fit 12–15 yoga participants. A cleared open-plan floor, terrace, or lawn works even better.

You don't need special flooring. Most participants bring their own mat or use towels. We can advise on this during the booking call.

What to Tell Employees Beforehand

Keep it simple. Employees need to know three things:

  1. Wear comfortable, stretchy clothing — or bring a change of clothes
  2. Avoid eating a heavy meal in the 2 hours before the session
  3. No prior yoga experience needed — the session is designed for all levels

That's it. We handle everything else.

How Long Is the Session?

Our standard corporate Yoga Day session is 60 minutes. This typically breaks down as:

  • 10 minutes: gentle warm-up and breath awareness
  • 35 minutes: asana sequence (adapted to all levels)
  • 10 minutes: Pranayama breathwork
  • 5 minutes: Savasana (guided relaxation)

Employees leave feeling noticeably different. Not tired — energised and calm simultaneously.
