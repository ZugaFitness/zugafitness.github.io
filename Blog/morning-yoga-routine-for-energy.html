7 Energising Poses to Do Every Morning

To get the most out of your morning yoga routine for energy, try this sequence designed to wake up every part of your body.

1. Cat-Cow (Marjaryasana-Bitilasana)

  • How to do it: Start on your hands and knees in a tabletop position. Inhale as you arch your back and look up (Cow), then exhale as you round your spine and tuck your chin (Cat).
  • Why it helps: This wakes up the spine and improves flexibility, helping to release the stiffness that accumulates during sleep.
  • Duration: Flow through 10 rounds of breath.

2. Child's Pose (Balasana)

  • How to do it: Kneel on the floor, sit back on your heels, and fold your torso forward over your thighs with your forehead on the mat.
  • Why it helps: It provides a gentle stretch for the hips and lower back while centering your mind for the day ahead.
  • Duration: Hold for 1 minute.

3. Downward-Facing Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana)

  • How to do it: From tabletop, tuck your toes and lift your hips toward the ceiling, forming an inverted "V" shape.
  • Why it helps: This pose energizes the full body by stretching the legs and back while increasing blood flow to the brain.
  • Duration: Hold for 1 minute.

4. Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana)

  • How to do it: Lie on your stomach, place your hands under your shoulders, and gently lift your chest off the floor while keeping your elbows close to your body.
  • Why it helps: It opens the chest and boosts lung capacity, making it an essential yoga to start the day with deeper breaths.
  • Duration: Hold for 30 seconds.

5. Warrior I (Virabhadrasana I)

  • How to do it: From a standing position, step one foot back into a lunge, turn your back heel down, and reach your arms toward the sky.
  • Why it helps: Warrior poses are designed for building focus and confidence, making them perfect for an energising yoga sequence.
  • Duration: 30 seconds per side.

6. Triangle Pose (Trikonasana)

  • How to do it: Stand with legs wide, reach one hand down toward your shin or a block, and the other toward the ceiling, creating a lateral stretch.
  • Why it helps: It improves balance and opens the sides of the body, which helps in increasing overall energy levels.
  • Duration: 30 seconds per side.

7. Tree Pose (Vrikshasana)

  • How to do it: Stand on one leg and place the sole of your other foot on your inner calf or thigh (avoiding the knee). Bring your hands to your heart.
  • Why it helps: This pose promotes stability and mindfulness, ensuring you stay focused as you begin your workday.
  • Duration: 30 seconds per side.

Takeaway: A balanced sequence that moves the spine, opens the chest, and challenges your balance is the most effective way to jumpstart your metabolism and mental clarity.
