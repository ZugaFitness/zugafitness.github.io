5 PCOS yoga poses That Target Symptoms Directly

These PCOS yoga poses are selected to help improve circulation to the pelvic region and calm the nervous system to support hormonal health.

  1. Butterfly Pose (Baddha Konasana):
    • How to do it: Sit with a tall spine, bring the soles of your feet together, and let your knees fall open. Hold your feet and gently pulse your knees.
    • Why it helps: Many practitioners report this pose helps stimulate the ovaries and improve blood flow to the reproductive organs, aiding in pelvic congestion relief.
    • Duration: Hold for 2–3 minutes.
  2. Supported Bridge Pose (Setu Bandhasana):
    • How to do it: Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat. Lift your hips and place a block or cushion under your sacrum for support.
    • Why it helps: Research suggests this inversion may help regulate the thyroid gland and stimulate abdominal organs, which is crucial for metabolic balance.
    • Duration: Hold for 1 minute.
  3. Reclining Bound Angle (Supta Baddha Konasana):
    • How to do it: Lie on your back in the butterfly leg position, supporting your knees with pillows if needed. Place one hand on your heart and one on your belly.
    • Why it helps: This restorative pose is excellent for reducing cortisol and allowing the pelvic floor to relax completely, which can support reproductive health.
    • Duration: 5–10 minutes.
  4. Seated Forward Fold (Paschimottanasana):
    • How to do it: Sit with legs outstretched and fold forward from the hips, reaching for your shins or feet.
    • Why it helps: Forward folds are known to calm the brain and help reduce stress and anxiety, which are often heightened in women with hormonal imbalances.
    • Duration: Hold for 1–2 minutes.
  5. Legs Up The Wall (Viparita Karani):
    • How to do it: Sit sideways against a wall and swing your legs up so they rest vertically against the wall while you lie on your back.
    • Why it helps: This pose is a game-changer for online yoga classes for PCOS because it helps in restoring energy and calming the adrenal glands by reversing blood flow.
    • Duration: 5–10 minutes.

Takeaway: Incorporating these five poses into your daily routine can help physically mitigate the stressors that disrupt your natural cycle.
